Summer Sublet Terms and Conditions

Payment, move-in / move-out video documentation, and security deposit rules

Identity Statement

I (Kate) am the current tenant and the person offering this room for summer sublet. Throughout this page, the terms “I,” “me,” “my,” and “mine” refer to me as the person subletting out the room. The terms “you,” “your,” and “tenant” refer to the person (Brenda) renting and staying in the room during the sublet period.

Overview

  • The rent is $1500 per month for a total of 2 months (June 5th to August 5th).
  • To secure the sublet, the tenant must pay $1500 (one month’s rent, included in the total total rental fee) upon confirming the agreement.
    • This payment is non-refundable once submitted.
  • On the move-in date (June 5th), after confirming move-in and submitting the move-in video, the tenant must pay:
    • Remaining rent for 1 months: $1500
    • Security deposit: $500 (fully refundable if no damage)
    • Total due on move-in day: $2000
  • The tenant is required to:
    • Record and send a move-in video upon arrival
    • Record and send a move-out video upon leaving
  • Further details are outlined below.

1. Rent and Reservation

  • The rent is $1500 per month for a total of 2 months.
  • To secure the sublet, you are required to pay $1500, equal to one month’s rent, when you decide to confirm this summer sublet with me.
  • This payment serves as a reservation payment to hold the room and confirm the arrangement.
  • Once the reservation payment is submitted, it is non-refundable if you cancel afterward.
  • This non-refundable rule exists because cancellation after confirmation may affect my schedule, timing, and ability to make other housing arrangements or find another tenant.

2. Key Handover

  • On the move-in date (June 5th), my roommate is expected to be present in the apartment. When you arrive, you are required to take and send me a photo showing that you have reached the apartment entrance, including the unit number.
  • After I receive your photo, I will immediately notify my roommate to let you in and hand over the key to you.
  • If my roommate is not present, I will arrange for another person to assist with the key handover.
  • For move-out, since I am not yet sure whether my roommate will be present at that time (August 5th), the key return arrangement will be communicated to you closer to your move-out date (August 5th). If my roommate is present, you will return the key to her, and send me a photo showing the key is left on the table or on her hand.

2. Move-in Process and Payment

  • The move-in date is June 5th.
  • After you move in, you must record a real-time move-in video of the room and apartment area listed in the video documentation section below.
  • You must send the move-in video to me promptly after moving in.
  • After I confirm that you have moved in and after I receive the move-in video, you are required to pay:
    • Remaining rent for the next one months: $1500
    • Security deposit: $500
  • The total payment due at this stage is $2000.
  • This $2000 payment is due on the move-in day after move-in confirmation and submission of the move-in video.

3. Video Documentation Requirement

  • You are required to provide video recordings at both:
    • Move-in
    • Move-out
  • Both videos must be recorded in real time and sent to me promptly.
  • Both videos must be recorded with sufficient lighting so that the condition of the room and apartment area can be clearly seen.
  • The videos must clearly show, including but not limited to:
    • Common areas:
      • Walls
      • Floors
      • Kitchen island
      • Built-in cabinets provided by the apartment
      • Refrigerator (both freezer and refrigerator compartments, open them to record)
      • Full bathroom condition
    • Your rented living area:
      • Walls
      • Floors
      • Windows and curtains
      • Air conditioning unit surface
  • These videos serve as the primary reference for verifying the condition of the room and apartment area at move-in and move-out.
  • Clear and complete video documentation is important for both parties to avoid misunderstandings or disputes regarding apartment damages that would cause any charges from the apartment building management.

4. Security Deposit

4.1 Deposit Amount and Payment Timing

  • A $500 security deposit is required at move-in.
  • This deposit is paid together with the remaining one months’ rent after I confirm that you have moved in and after I receive the move-in video.

4.2 Return Condition

  • The security deposit will be returned after move-out, subject to no damages beyond normal wear and tear.
  • If there is no damage beyond normal wear and tear, the security deposit will be returned in full.

4.3 Return Timing

  • Your expected move-out date is August 5th.
  • I may not return to the apartment until after August 20th.
  • Because of this, the condition of the room and apartment area will be evaluated after I return to the apartment.
  • I will share my return flight information with you for transparency.
  • Once I enter the apartment after returning, I will immediately record a video of the room and relevant apartment area and send it to you.
  • The security deposit will be returned after I complete the inspection, subject to the damage evaluation standards listed below.

4.4 Damage Assessment Standard

Any damage will only be assessed based on the comparison between the move-in and move-out videos. I will not attribute any issues unless they are clearly shown in the video comparison.
  • Damage responsibility will be determined based on the comparison between the move-in video and the move-out video.
  • I will not hold you responsible for any issue that is not clearly supported by the video comparison.
  • This means a clear, complete, and well-lit video recording of the apartment condition is very important.
  • The purpose of this standard is to make the damage assessment fair and based on visible evidence, rather than assumption.

4.5 Damage Repair and Deposit Deduction

  • If there is clear damage in my living area that is attributable to you based on the comparison between the move-in and move-out videos, I will notify the apartment building management for repair.
  • Any repair cost charged by the apartment or building management for such confirmed damage will be deducted from the security deposit.
  • If the confirmed repair cost is less than the security deposit, the remaining balance will be returned to you.
  • If no qualifying damage is confirmed, the security deposit will be returned in full.
  • No need to worry about the damage of my personal stuff, such as my bed, my table, my chair, etc, since the apartment building management will not charge me for any damage to my personal stuff. So I will also NOT charge you for any damage to my personal stuff.

4.6 Purpose of the Security Deposit and Video Evidence

  • The security deposit is intended to cover potential damage beyond normal wear and tear.
  • The move-in and move-out videos protect both parties by creating a clear record of the room and apartment condition.
  • The video requirement is not meant to create unnecessary difficulty, but to make the process transparent and fair for both sides.

Thank you for your reading, understanding and cooperation! Hope you have a great summer!
